I'm Callum Morrison, a design engineering graduate from the University of Bath, and in the summer of 2021 2022 (thanks to COVID), I am embarking on the greatest motoring adventure on the planet * - The Mongol Rally, alongside my good mate, Jack van Galen.

* second only to the M25 at rush hour.


This entails driving our (t)rusty 1.2 litre lunchbox from Oxford, UK all the way to Georgia (home of the cave city of Vardzia, the Greater Caucasus Mountain Range, and some bears).

The Mongol Rally has been an annual event since 2004, raising money for charity ever since it's first rally with only six teams. Since then, it has grown to around 300 teams every year, and in 2022, that cohort includes us for the first time.

In previous years, the rally finish line was all the way over in Mongolia (hence the name), but due to various geopolitical and global-pandemical reasons it's a bit different now. That doesn't mean it won't be a monumental challenge, where calamities and shenanigans are guaranteed.

But, to do this, we need your help


There are two areas you can get involved:



1. Fundraising for Ukraine.

The Disaster Emergency Committee is the official charity of this year's Mongol Rally. These guys are doing a brilliant job of putting money into the hands of trusted organisations in a way that gets help to where it’s needed fast.

 

- £50 could provide blankets for 4 families to keep them warm
- £100 could provide emergency food for 5 people for one month
- £150 could provide blankets for 12 families to keep them warm
- £300 could provide emergency food for 6 families for one month

2. Sponsorship for the journey.

Want to help us reach the finish line?   There are loads of ways of doing this, maybe you would like your shiny company logo displayed proudly on our bonnet for all to see, and in return you have some products or services that might help us?  Or maybe you have some old jerry cans lying around, which you meant to throw out three years ago, but they might just stop us from getting stranded in the middle of nowhere?
